India

Geography

  1. India is situated north of the equator.
  2. It is the seventh-largest country in the world, with a total area of 3,287,263 sq.km.
  3. On the south, India has Indian Ocean, on the west there is Arabian Sea, the Lakshadweep Sea to the southwest, the Bay of Bengal on the east, and the Indian Ocean proper to the south.

History

India’s history is rich and diverse

Period Time Frame Key Events and Characteristics
Indus Valley Civilization 2600–1900 BCE Flourished in present-day Pakistan and northwestern India.
Aryan Invasion 1500–500 BCE Migration of Indo-European people.
Mauryan Empire 322 BCE - 6th centuries CE Established by Chandragupta; significant expansion and governance.
Classical Age Gupta Empire witnessed a Hindu resurgence; advancements in arts and sciences.
Mughal Empire 1530–1720 Period of economic expansion, relative peace, and patronage of the arts.
Maratha Empire 1674-1818 Ruled my Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Demography

India, the world’s second-most populated country, after China to become the most populated nation in 2023. It has over 1.4 billion people, which is more than the entire population of Europe.

India’s demographics are incredibly diverse, with over two thousand ethnic groups, representing at least six major religions and over 100 spoken languages

Mumbai

Geography

Mumbai, was also known as Bombay, it is a fantastic city with a rich geographical history.

  1. It is situated on the the west coast of India, along the Konkan coast.
  2. Mumbai is a landmass that is formed by 7 sets of islands.
  3. This islands are Isle of Bombay, Parel, Mazagaon, Mahim, Colaba, Worli, and Old Woman's Island.

History

  1. Mumbai was a crucial port city under British rule. The early 1900s saw the construction of important infrastructure such as the Gateway of India, completed in 1924
  2. The textile mills were the backbone of Mumbai’s economy, employing thousands and driving urbanization.
  3. Mumbai became the epicenter of India’s entertainment industry, popularly known as Bollywood, which gained prominence in the 1970s and 1980s.
  4. Mumbai solidified its position as India’s financial, commercial, and entertainment hub, with the Bombay Stock Exchange, Reserve Bank of India, and numerous multinational corporations headquartered here.

Demography

Mumbai also called as City of Dreams.City is named after godess Mumbadevi

  1. Mumbai's population is approximately 20 million.
  2. The major ethnic groups include Marathis, Gujaratis, North Indians, South Indians, and people from various other states.
  3. Marathi is the official language of Mumbai and is widely spoken by the local population. Hindi is also commonly spoken and understood.

Gateway of India

What It Is ?

Gateway of India is an monument which was made in 1924 on the coastal area of Mumbai. It is a major tourist hub in the city. This monument was buid to welcome King Georgy 5 and Queen Mary at Apollo Bunder(dock).

Location

The Gateway of India is an iconic landmark in Mumbai, Maharashtra state. It is located on the waterfront at an angle, opposite the Taj Mahal Palace and Tower Hotel. It is situated at the end of Chhatrapati Shivaji Marg in South Mumbai and overlooks the Arabian Sea.

Why Visit ?

  • This place is if you come from the seaways, also an entrance to India.
  • It is one of the famous tourist spot of Mumbai.The area around the Gateway is bustling with activity.
  • You can find street vendors, food stalls, and local artists. It is an excellent place to soak in the local culture.
  • You can take boat rides to Elephanta Caves or explore nearby islands
